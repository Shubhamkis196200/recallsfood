# 10 Most Common Foodborne Pathogens and How to Avoid Them

1. **Norovirus** — ~19-21 million cases/year. Wash hands, wash produce.
2. **Salmonella** — ~1.35 million cases/year. Cook poultry to 165°F.
3. **Clostridium perfringens** — ~1 million cases/year. Keep hot foods above 140°F.
4. **Campylobacter** — ~1.5 million cases/year. Cook poultry thoroughly.
5. **Staphylococcus aureus** — ~240,000 cases/year. Proper hand washing.
6. **E. coli (STEC)** — ~265,000 cases/year. Cook ground beef to 160°F.
7. **Listeria monocytogenes** — ~1,600 cases/year (very serious). Avoid high-risk foods if vulnerable.
8. **Toxoplasma** — ~87,000 cases/year. Cook meat thoroughly.
9. **Cyclospora** — varies. Wash imported produce.
10. **Hepatitis A** — ~2,500 cases/year. Get vaccinated.
